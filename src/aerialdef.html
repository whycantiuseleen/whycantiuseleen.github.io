THE PROJECT

BACKGROUND

Aerial Defence is an interactive multiplayer game developed by taking inspiration from the 1930s pencil and paper game "The Battleships". It is a digital remake with the introduction of role-playing characters that is designed to hold unique abilities and it is also a competitive multiplayer turn based game.

GOALS

  • Introduce strategic thinking into the original luck-based game.
  • Intuitive game design for new players to easily assimilate into the game.

PROCESS

01 IDEATION

Since the original "Battleships" was mainly a luck-based game, the team wanted to remake the game in order to introduce strategic thinking elements so as to make the game more diverse and exciting for the players. We wanted to provide game mechanics that will allow players to have a totally new experience for each round of gameplay as they will have to weigh the pros and cons of each action that is taken.

From there, we decided to develop a RPG which will introduce randomness and hence multiple approaches towards each round. Each character that was introduced into the game have specific roles and abilities that will significantly turn the tables of the game at any point.

02 DESIGN

I mainly worked on the interaction design of the game and also collaborated with the visual artist of our team, Ken Jyi, for the theme and character designs.

INTERACTION DESIGN

Some considerations that I had when designing for the user's experience include the fact that the game is hosted on a mobile platform. Coupled with our survey results whereby the majority of the possible playerbase will usually play this game while on the go. This means that we had to consider for the fact that a single-handed navigation would be more suitable unless there is too much controls required which was the reason behind the usage of portrait mode for the game's orientation.

From our initial user-testing, we also observed that most people were unsure of their course-of-action due to the lack of pointers and subtle guides. As such, prompts were added when the system detects that players have been in idle mode for a period of time. The prompts also helped in the onboarding process as it educates and reminds players of the abilities that each character has.

EVALUATION

During the exhibition that was opened to the public, we observed that most individuals could easily pick up the game after a brief introduction on the basic game mechanics of the original game. 3 out of 10 individuals went on to play their second game which is considered successful as most gamers will give up on a game quickly if it was difficult for them to navigate.

At the end of the project, we were able to implement most of the modules that were planned out for except for the character leveling function due to the time constraint that the team faced.
