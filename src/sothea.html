THE PROJECT

BACKGROUND

Due to the political unrest in Cambodia, adminstration of the national record was not properly done during those periods and hence there is a lack of centralised national records for identification. This makes the medical health screenings in Cambodian villages highly inefficient as numerous man hours are often wasted on the retrieval of medical histories as well as on identifying both new and revisiting patients. To alleviate this problem, a medical database was set up by SUTD Makerspace with an accompanying web application for the NUS medical students who provides free health screenings for the Cambodians annually.

However, there were negative feedbacks from the medical students regarding the web application and hence the team will be looking into redesigning this web application to help ease their pain points.

GOALS

  • Reduce the time taken for the Medical Screenings
  • Increase Doctor-Patient Interaction during the Doctor's Consultation
  • Reduce User Cognitive Load

PROCESS

01 DISCOVER

To understand about our user's pain-points, we have gathered their feedback and also analyzed the existing user interface.

"Cambodian patients usually have very short-attention span and hence there are many that dropped out halfway through the screening since it takes too long for us to screen them and fill in the forms."

- NUS Medical Student

"The interface is messy and unintuitive as information is all over the place. It takes too long for us to obtain the information for diagnosis to be made."

- NUS Medical Student

Areas for improvements were also identified in the existing UI which includes the following:

  • Multiple regions of possible user-driven error
    e.g. clear-all button is currently placed right beside the submit button
  • Graphic design concerns: proper alignment, colors and rearrangement of field
    e.g. visual hierarchy, color indication gives off the wrong signals.
  • Prioritizing of information

    • 02 ANALYSIS

    Since there were feedback regarding the dropouts during the screening process due to the short-attention span that the Cambodians have, one of our project goals was hence to shorten the total time taken for the whole screening process. This means that we had to focus on shortening the time taken for the form-filling in medical stations and to increase Doctor-Patient interaction so that the patients will feel engaged in the consultation sessions. We also aimed to reduce cognitive load for the users, allowing them to focus better for each session.

    Two different user flows were identified for this project:

  • Medical staffs who are assigned to the 4 different medical stations.
  • Doctors who are in charge of carrying out the consultation sessions.

    • User Journey Map based on feedbacks given

    03 WIREFRAMES/MOCK-UP

    For this project, I mainly focused on designing for the consultation sessions together with another teammate, Tessa. We would generate design ideas and conduct critic sessions together with the rest of the team in order to refine the design.

    ITERATION 1

    Patient details screen

    In our first iteration, both of us generated our own initial ideas on the UI styleguide and also identified the main features to be displayed for the doctor's user flow. For me, I have identified the following features to be displayed:

    • Patient Card
    • Medical History
    • Actionables and summary of completion status
    The motive was to mimic a usual clinic visit experience whereby the doctor will first read through the patient details and medical history before starting a new session.

    Consultation screen

    The consultation page has a tabbed session which allows the doctor to easily tap back to obtain the screening results of each session each time.

    To reduce user-driven errors that were pointed out, friction was introduced into the experience by positioning the clear-all button on the other side

    Graphic design concerns were also addressed as the different sections are properly demarcated and the elements are properly aligned.

    Although user-driven errors and graphic design concerns were mostly addressed in this iteration, we noticed that both our designs did not address the goal of decreasing the doctor's focus on the screen as we realized that the touchpoints for the different call-to-actions were too small and there were still too much information that the doctors have to digest which meant that they will focus more on the screen instead of their patients.

    ITERATION 2

    In our second iteration, we focused more on prioritization of information. This led to the usage of contrasting colors for diagnosis results such that there is a reduction in the need to process raw data, reducing cognitive load for the users. Also the contrasting color makes it easier for the user to pinpoint the important information at one glance.

    Patient Page for Doctors - Iteration 2

    To help reduce user's need to focus on the sreen, we explored blurring inactive sections so that the users can easily pinpoint the sections that they need to focus on while drawing lessons from a popular form-filling widget, Typeform. However, due to the usage of iPads/tablets, the blurred out sections can still be seen as it does not solve the issue of small call-to-actions.

    Consultation Screen - Iteration 2

    FINAL PRODUCT

    DESIGN

    The final design that we came up with introduced gestures for navigation which maximizes the touchpoint surface area as compared to a small button. Users can swipe to navigate between pages instead of the usual "Next" or "Back" buttons. This allows us to remove these elements from the screen and maximizes the questionare buttons allowing users to fully focus on interacting with their patient with minimal attention needed to be spent on the tablet itself.

    Also, the whole consultation will be recorded and transcribed into text through the speech-to-text technology and this will be saved in the medical history of the patient.

    However, we also noticed that certain important features cannot be fully replaced such as the additional notes and comments from the doctors. We understand from our user's perspective that there are situations whereby they will have some pointers to be keyed into the system and this cannot be replaced by the speech-to-text function as they might not want to disclose this to the patient. As such a textbox is retained for the doctors to enter short consultation notes.

    EVALUATION

    During testing, it is observed that the transition from laptop to tablet interface proved to be a significant improvement. The tablet based UI design, mainly the input changes from text inputs to buttons with large tap areas played a huge role in the improvement.

    Statistical results above shows that the amount of eye contact with the patient had increased on average by 12% which fulfills our goal of increasing doctor patient interaction.
